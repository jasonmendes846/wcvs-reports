**Methodology Notes:**
- Estimates are **nominal annualized total returns** (price appreciation + dividends)
- Based on Shiller CAPE regression, Hussman Strategic Growth model, GMO 7-year forecasts, and Bogle's expected return formula: Return = Dividend Yield + EPS Growth ± PE Change

- Confidence intervals widen at shorter horizons due to sentiment/momentum noise

**Historical Context:**
- CAPE 41x has only occurred 3 times: 1929, 2000, 2021. Subsequent 10Y returns: ~−1% to +2% nominal.
- The 2021 episode (CAPE 38–40) was followed by 2022 drawdown (−19.4%), then AI-driven recovery — showing forward returns depend heavily on earnings growth sustainability.
- Current 2026 consensus EPS growth of +14% is the wildcard. If realized, 1Y returns could exceed upper bound. If missed, lower bound breaks.

## Key Drivers & What's Changed

### Bearish Factors (Pressuring Score Lower)
1. **CAPE at 41x** — Third-highest reading in history (behind 2021 and 2000). Mean reversion risk is extreme.
2. **Buffett Indicator at 201%** — Market cap nearly double US GDP. Only 2021 was higher.
3. **Record margin debt** — Investors are levered to the gills. A 10% correction would trigger forced selling.
4. **Put/Call complacency** — At 0.46, puts are being sold into strength. No hedges = no safety net.
5. **P/S at 3.6x** — Even 2000 dot-com peak was "only" ~2.5x. This is uncharted territory.

### Bullish Factors (Preventing Lower Score)
1. **Breadth is strong** — NH/NL 8.3:1, A/D line positive. This isn't a "7 stocks carrying the market" setup.
2. **Credit spreads tight** — HY at 2.83%. Bond market sees no recession, no default wave.
3. **Yield curve re-steepened** — +51bps removes the 2025 recession signal. "Higher for longer" is manageable.
4. **Earnings growth** — 2026 EPS consensus ~$306 (+14% YoY). AI capex ($400B from hyperscalers) is real revenue.
5. **Fed posture** — Market pricing 2-3 cuts in H2 2026. "Dovish pivot" narrative alive.

### Neutral / Mixed
- **Forward P/E at 20.9x** — High, but not absurd if 14% EPS growth materializes. The market is "paying up for growth."
- **Tobin's Q at 1.17** — Above replacement cost, but driven by intangible assets (AI, software, IP). Traditional Q may understate modern corporate value.
